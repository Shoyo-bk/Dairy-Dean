📋 Park's Standards

Read carefully before you start — these standards guide every zone!

🌾 Zone 1 — Hygiene Rules

  • Milk must come from healthy udders only
  • Animal premises must be clean and sanitary
  • Milk handlers must be free of disease
  • Use sterile pails with tight lids for collection
  • No carriers of typhoid or streptococcal disease allowed

🔵 Zone 2 — Methylene Blue Reduction Test

  • Decolorisation in less than 30 minutes → Very Poor quality
  • Decolorisation in 30 to 120 minutes → Poor quality
  • Decolorisation in 2 to 5 hours → Satisfactory quality
  • Stays blue for more than 5 hours → Good to Excellent quality

🔥 Zone 3 — Pasteurisation Methods

  • Holder (Vat) Method: heat to 63–66°C and hold for 30 minutes
  • High Temperature Short Time (HTST): heat to 72°C and hold for 15 seconds
  • Ultra High Temperature (UHT): heat to 125°C and hold for 2–4 seconds
  • After any method, cool milk immediately to 4°C or below
  • Cooling to above 4°C allows microorganisms to grow

🔬 Zone 4 — Tests for Adequate Pasteurisation

  • Phosphatase test: must be Negative — clear or colourless = pass ✅; yellow = fail ❌
  • Standard Plate Count: 30,000 or fewer organisms per millilitre
  • Coliform count: Zero per 1 millilitre

🔬 Zone 4: Tests for Adequate Pasteurisation

Examine each test result carefully — pass or reject each one!

📖 Park's Quality Standards for Pasteurised Milk:
• Phosphatase Test: Must be Negative — clear or colourless tube = adequate pasteurisation ✅; yellow tube = pasteurisation failed ❌
• Standard Plate Count: Must be 30,000 or fewer organisms per millilitre
• Coliform Count: Must be Zero per 1 millilitre
